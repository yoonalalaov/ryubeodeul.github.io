Before shape, there is fabric. Before silhouette, there is surface.
My work began in textiles - learning how material holds light, tension, and movement.
That foundation continues in the way I design now.
Every garment begins with touch.
Structure follows. Image completes it.

Now, more than ever, we need space to explore human feeling
— beyond speed, beyond automation, beyond the noise of trends and
instant gratification. Fashion can easily become repetition.

Trends predict. Algorithms calculate. But clothing is worn by real bodies,
in real moments, under changing light, weather, and emotion.
I believe design should go beyond what is expected.

Not louder, not exaggerated — but intentional, attentive, and alive.
Every choice in proportion, fabric, and form matters; every seam, fold,
and detail carries weight in how the garment is experienced.

I pay attention to proportion, movement, and surface —
to how a garment holds its shape, stretches, folds, and shifts
as someone moves through space. I combine the familiar
with the unfamiliar, structure with softness,
precision with slight imperfection.

I consider how clothes feel over time, how they respond to gesture,
posture, and rhythm, how they invite interaction and discovery.
If everyone likes it instantly, it may be too safe.
Design, for me, is a dialogue between intention and experience.
What we wear should not feel generated. It should feel considered, lived-in, and resonant.
